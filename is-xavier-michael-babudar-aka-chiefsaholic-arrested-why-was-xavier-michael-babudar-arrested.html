Authorities have arrested Xavier Michael Babudar, also known as “ChiefsAholic,” after he evaded capture following his initial arrest for an alleged bank robbery in Bixby, Oklahoma.

They apprehended Babudar in California on Friday.

The FBI announced the arrest on Monday and charged Babudar in federal court with bank theft and transporting stolen property across state lines.

Multiple branches of the FBI collaborated in the joint investigation leading to the arrest.

Babudar, a well-known superfan of the Kansas City Chiefs, gained recognition for his distinctive wolf costume.

He frequently wore it to games and showcased it on social media.

Babudar is also accused of committing a series of robberies at various banks and credit unions throughout the Midwest.

He bought and redeemed a significant number of chips from different casinos in Missouri, Kansas, and Illinois between April and December 2022.

What Happened to Xavier Michael Babudar?

Xavier Michael Babudar, also known as “ChiefsAholic,” successfully evaded authorities as a fugitive for four months.

However, they finally apprehended him in California on Friday. Currently, he is facing legal consequences for his actions and has been charged in federal court.

The charges against him include bank theft and transporting stolen property across state lines.

Babudar’s arrest was the outcome of a multi-state investigation that involved authorities from Oklahoma City, Minneapolis, Omaha, California, and Memphis.
