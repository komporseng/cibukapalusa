Who is Kathy Najimy?

Kathy Najimy is an American actress and comedian who has won an Obie Award in 1989. She is also known as a gay and lesbian rights fighter. Kathy is popular as a Television actress as well as a big-screen actress.

She is best known for her role as Olive Massery in the TV series Victoria’s Closet (1997-2000) and as Sister Mary Patrick in the 1992 American comedy film Sister Act.

Kathy Najimy: Age, Parents, Ethnicity

Kathy Najimy was born on February 6, 1957, in San Diego, California. As of 2021, she is 64 years old. She belongs to a Lebanese ethnicity and an American nationality.

She is the daughter of Samia Massery (mother) and Fred Najimy(father). Her father was a postal worker.

Education

Kathy Najimy: Professional Life and Career

Kathy Najimy began her career as an actor with several minor roles in the early 1990s movies and TV films. Her major role came up as Sister Mary Patrick in the 1992 comedy film Sister Act. She also appeared in the lead role in Sister Act 2 in 1993.

Later, Kathy starred in Hocus Pocus as Sanderson’s sister. In 1997, she worked as a voice artist in a video game of Warner Bros: Family Entertainment.

In 1998, she did a cameo role in Bride of Chucky. She co-starred in the 2001 hit comedy movie Rat Race.

Kathy Najimy created and starred in the hit HBO show Kathy and Mo alongside her friend Mo Gaffney, an actress.

She is famous for her voice as the drama teacher, Miss Thespius in the 1998 hit Disney series, Hercules. Since then, Kathy has appeared in several TV series and movies and she has also worked as a voice-over artist.

In 2015, she appeared in the role of Evil Queen in Disney’s Descendants. Kathy has been a Gay and Lesbian rights fighter since she was 18. She has also raised her voice for people’s rights and democracy.
